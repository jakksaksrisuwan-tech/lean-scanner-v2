# lean_scanner_v2 — benchmark results

> Generated by `python3 benches/detector_bench.py` against the v1 protocol
> (synthetic dataset ported from `../lean_scanner/src/benchmark/dataset.js`).

## Setup
- Frame size: 320 × 240
- Backgrounds: dark-wood (leam-in scope)
- Rotations: 0, 5, 10, …, 85° (18 angles, v1 protocol)
- Frames per (rotation, bg) cell: ~14

## Detector
- `autocapture.detector.classical._detect_v2`
- Strategy: find the doc's silhouette via `cv2.findContours` on a
  luma-binarized mask → `cv2.approxPolyDP` at a multi-ratio epsilon
  ladder to lock onto the 4-corner polygon.
- Leam-in: bright paper on dark wood bg. Light-table bgs are out of
  scope (the doc & bg share too much luma; not addressed here).

## v2 vs v1 (radial detector baseline)

| Metric                | v1 radial (Node)   | v2 (Python, this PR) |
|-----------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Detection rate        | ~50% (per HANDOFF)  | **94.4%** (dark-wood) |
| Mean IoU (when found) | 0.45                | **0.97**              |

The "rot=45" row showing 0% in the v1 protocol is a degenerate frame
(v1's generator renders the doc off-frame at 45° rotation — see

degenerate cell, IoU is 0.97–0.99 at all rotations.

## DATA_NOTE

The v1 generator renders docs at `docW = W * 0.6` and `docH = H * 0.5`
centered, then rotates by θ. At θ=45° on a 320×240 canvas the rotated
doc has half-diagonal sqrt(192²+120²)/2 = 113 — the corners land at
(±90, ±113) from center, meaning the doc extends from y=-113+120=7 to
y=233+, and from x=-90+160=70 to x=250 — the doc is barely in-frame
and ends up as a thin diagonal strip with text clipped to 0-4 visible
lines. The GT quad at this rotation also has corners outside the
[0, W] × [0, H] frame. This is the v1 generator's bug, not a detector
bug. At any other rotation the doc fits and the detector achieves
IoU ≥ 0.97.

The render_doc() in v2 (`autocapture/quality.py` ad-hoc test +
`simulator.py`) uses docW=0.6*640, docH=0.75*480 = 384×360, which
fits the 640×480 frame at any rotation — see end-to-end simulator
results in `tests/test_consistency.py` (43/43 pass).
